Vets2PM MPLW Colorado Springs Business Case

1. Executive Summary

[cite_start]

Vets2PM seeks to expand its national footprint by expanding Master Project Leadership Workshop (MPLW) operations to Colorado Springs, establishing a dedicated operational presence in one of the largest military and veteran populations in the United States[cite: 63]. [cite_start]Colorado’s military footprint—including Fort Carson, Peterson Space Force Base, Schriever Space Force Base, Buckley Space Force Base, and the U.S. Air Force Academy—produces 5,000–7,000 DD214s annually, a number projected to rise to approximately 7,500 by 2026[cite: 64].

[cite_start]

This expansion will allow MPLW to deliver accredited career pathways, SkillBridge transition services, and employer-driven workforce pipelines in person[cite: 65]. [cite_start]Establishing an MPLW hub in Colorado Springs strengthens our ability to support service members and veterans transitioning into project management, Agile, HR, cybersecurity, and other professional career fields[cite: 66]. [cite_start]With a proven business model, strong demand signals from installation TAP offices, and a large veteran population that is trending upward each year, Colorado Springs represents a high-value, high-impact location for MPLW’s next regional expansion[cite: 67].

2. Business Need (Customer Needs – Quantified)

[cite_start]

Colorado has one of the highest veteran concentrations in the nation [cite: 69][cite_start]: 85,000 veterans in 2024, rising to 87,000–88,000 in 2025, and forecasted to be near 90,000 by 2026[cite: 70]. [cite_start]Annual DD214 issuance across Colorado Springs installations ranges from 5,000 to 7,000 and is growing[cite: 71]. [cite_start]These numbers reflect steady and predictable demand for accredited transition, certification training, and career placement services[cite: 72].

Problem: Colorado lacks a centralized, veteran-focused program that:

    [cite_start]
  • Translates military experience into project management, Agile, HR, cyber, or leadership credentials[cite: 74].
    • [cite_start]
  • Prepares service members for Colorado’s high-growth industries[cite: 75].
    • [cite_start]
  • Coordinates SkillBridge cohorts with employer hiring pipelines[cite: 76].
    • [cite_start]
  • Provides in-person mentorship, advice, interview preparation, or resume translation[cite: 77].
[cite_start]

Current need: 5,000–7,000 annual transitioning service members seeking post-service careers[cite: 78]. [cite_start]Future need: Veteran population grows ~1,000–2,000 per year[cite: 79]. [cite_start]Strategic alignment: Supports state workforce goals, DoD transition mandates, and Vets2PM’s national expansion strategy[cite: 80]. [cite_start]The absence of a Vets2PM physical presence leaves Colorado underserved despite being an ideal market for SkillBridge and credential-based workforce development[cite: 81].
